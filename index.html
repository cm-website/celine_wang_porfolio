Meet Celine Wang

I present myself in words, languages, and the quiet moments of creation.

First, in journalism. I developed my own rhythm of observation and storytelling through The Chronicle, my school newspaper, and the Junior Classical League, where I served as the State Editor for the California newspaper, the Nuntius. I wrote and arranged over fifteen stories that mirrored the conversations of my community. Each article became an act of transforming experience into meaning, a connection between what I see and what I feel.

In poetry, I found another language. This language bends, pauses, and breathes. Through the breaks of verses, I learned to articulate emotions, often fusing Latin structures with contemporary imagery to explore how words can liberate thought. My independent research on Latin and democracy extended that search for understanding, showing how ancient voices shaped the ideas of freedom and civic virtue that still come into action today.

Through Aretas, a Latin website I created, I solidified this fascination. As the founder of Aretas, I blended classical language with a modern, accessible design to make ancient texts tangible again. It became both a project and a reflection. It was a place where my curiosity met with my collaboration with my community, and where my love for learning transformed into something others could experience.
